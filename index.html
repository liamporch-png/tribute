Valerie Thomas

Valerie Thomas is a black, female scientist that invented the 3D illusion transmitter, or more commonly known as the hologram.

Valerie Thomas was born on Februrary 8th, 1943. At an early age, she was interested in mathematics, physics, and electronics even though, women weren't supposed to study those subjects at the time. Despite segregated standards at the time, she still got a good eucation. She soon attended Morgan State College (now Morgan State University), and graduated in 1864 with a bachelor's degree in physics.

Not too long later, she started working as a data analyst at NASA, and one of her first jobs was analysing data from the Orbiting Geophysical Observatory. After that, she worked on Landsat, an unmanned satelite program dedicated to studying Earth's natural resources.

In 1976, she got interested in 3D illusions after seeing a demonstration in hich a light bulb seemed to stay lit after it was removed. The illusion in question involved concave mirrors reflecting another image of a lightbulb. Her inventon took inspiration from this, and instead of a single image it now uses a film strip to display the image as if it was moving.
